The Heroes of HIMACHAL PRADESH

Naik Jadunath Singh

Born : 21 November 1916(Khajuri, Shahjahanpur, British India)
Died : 6 February 1948 (Tain Dhar, Nowshera, Jammu and Kashmir)
Years of service : 1941–1948
Rank : Naik
Unit : 1st Battalion, Rajput Regiment
Awards : Param Vir Chakra

Naik Jadunath Singh was an Indian soldier who was posthumously awarded the Param Vir Chakra, India's highest military decoration, for his actions in an engagement during the Indo-Pakistani War of 1947–1948. Born in Shahjahanpur, Singh enlisted in the British Indian Army in 1941 and served during World War II, fighting against Japanese forces in the Burma campaign. He subsequently participated in the Indo-Pakistani War of 1947 as a member of the newly established Indian Army. For his actions on 6 February 1948 at Tain Dhar, to the north of Naushahra, Singh was awarded the Param Vir Chakra Singh commanded a nine-man forward section post. Though heavily outnumbered by advancing Pakistani forces, Singh led his men in defending against three attempts to overtake the post. He was wounded during the second assault. Armed with a Sten gun, he single-handedly charged the third assault with such determination as to cause the attackers to withdraw. In doing so, he was killed. ....

Havildar Abdul Hamid

Born : 1 July 1933 (Dhamupur, Ghazipur district,British India)
Died : 10 September 1965 (Chima, Khem Karan, Tarn Taran district, Punjab, India)
Years of service : 1954–1965
Rank : Company Quartermaster Havildar
Unit : 4 Grenadiers
Awards : Param Vir Chakra, Samar Seva Medal, Raksha Medal,Sainya Seva Medal

Company Quartermaster Havildar Abdul Hamid Idrisi, was an Indian soldier. He was posthumously given India's highest military decoration, the Param Vir Chakra, for his actions during the Indo-Pakistani War of 1965.Hamid joined the army in December 1954, and was posted to the 4th Battalion of the Grenadiers regiment. During the Sino-Indian War, his battalion participated in the battle of Namka Chu against the Chinese People's Liberation Army. During the Indo-Pakistani War of 1965, the 4 Grenadiers battalion was entrusted with a vital position before the village of Chima on the Khem Karan–Bhikhiwind line. At the Battle of Asal Uttar on 9–10 September 1965, Hamid destroyed eight Pakistani tanks and was killed in action destroying the ninth tank.....

General Arun Shridhar Vaidya

Born 27 July 1926 (Kolaba District,Maharashtra, British India)
Died 10 August 1986 (Pune, Maharashtra, India)
Years of service 1944 - 1986
Rank : General
Unit : 9th Deccan Horse
Awards : Padma Vibhushan (posthumous), Param Vishisht Seva Medal, Maha Vir Chakra

General Arunkumar Shridhar Vaidya was a General Officer in the Indian Army. Vaidya received an emergency commission in the Indian Armoured Corps on 20 October 1945, with the service number IEC-11597, and received a regular army commission as a lieutenant on 7 May 1947 (seniority from 20 April 1947).In September 1948, Vaidya was involved in Operation Polo as a member of the ad hoc armoured force organised from the Armoured Corps Centre and School. The force captured the Daulatabad Fort, the Ellora Caves area and Parbhani. In 1958, he was selected to attend the Defence Services Staff College, Wellington, having secured a competitive vacancy. He was subsequently appointed brigade major of the 70 Infantry Brigade. He served in this appointment in Ladakh during the Sino-Indian War in 1962 He served as the 13th Chief of the Army Staff from 1983 to 1986. a few months before India's independence.Following his retirement, he was assassinated by Harjinder Singh Jinda and Sukhdev Singh Sukha in August 1986. Brigadier Sant Singh

Born : 12 July 1921 (Panjgrain Kalan, Faridkot, Punjab)
Died : 9 December 2015 (Sahibzada Ajit Singh Nagar)
Years of service : 16 February 1947 - 1973
Rank : Brigadier
Unit : Sikh Light Infantry
Awards : Maha Vir Chakra Bar

Brig Sant Singh, whom the freedom fighters in 1971 fondly called “Brigadier Babaji” because of his attire – typical Sikh turban and beard and affectionate attitude, was an officer in the Indian Army. He was one of the six officers of the Indian Army to have been twice decorated with the Maha Vir Chakra, India's second highest war time military decoration. Singh was commissioned into the Sikh Light Infantry on 16 February 1947 on a short-service commission, receiving a regular commission in the Indian Army as a lieutenant on 15 August 1951.He took over as the commander of the Sikh Light Infantry in 1964 and led the regiment to victory in the Battle of OP Hill in the Indo-Pakistani War of 1965. He remained as the commander of the regiment till 1968. He was promoted to acting brigadier and given command of an infantry brigade on 28 August 1969. On 10 March 1972, Singh was given command of an infantry brigade. He retired on 12 July 1973 after 26 years of service. He was one of the Indian officers involved in training the Mukti Bahini, during the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971 and Bangladesh Liberation War.........
